# Circumbinary Planets ~ Joshua Winn
- 2010 - first evidence of circuminary planets --> by Kepler Space Telescope --> Kepler 16
- The stars eclipse each other, the planets as well. --> everything we know about ciircumbinary systems is by studying the eclipses
- In a circumbinary system, we can't put the planets close to the stars --> cus it would be unstable (orbits)
  - however outside the radius of instability we find planets often
- how circumbinary planets form is related to how binary stars form in the first place.
- **Kepler-47** hosts multiple planets
  - which means Binary stars could host planetary systems like our solar system (>8 planets)
